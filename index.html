Common questions

The straight answers, before you book.

If you've made it this far, you're weighing things up. Here's what most people ask before saying yes.

How long does implementation actually take?
Most field service businesses are live in four to six weeks. The first two weeks are configuration and data migration. The rest is workflow build, training and a soft go live. Your dedicated Customer Success Manager runs the whole thing alongside you.
What happens to the data we already have in our current systems?
Customer records, jobs, invoices, sites and contacts all migrate across as part of onboarding. We've handled migrations from Sage, Xero, QuickBooks, every major scheduling tool and a lot of homegrown Excel. If you're worried about a specific source, ask in your demo.
Will my engineers actually use it?
JobWatch is the bit that lives on the engineer's tablet. It's been refined over a decade with field operatives in mind, runs offline, and is genuinely faster than paper for routine jobs. Most teams pick it up inside a shift. Holdouts usually come round once they see invoicing happen on the day.
Is there a contract minimum?
Standard contracts run 36 months, which gives our team the runway to deliver real ROI rather than just sell software. Most customers see payback inside the first quarter. We'll walk through the maths in your demo.
What does support actually look like at 2am on a Sunday?
Our RoadCrew customer service team is in Leeds, available 24/7, and answered by real humans. Standard onboarding tier includes business hours phone, the Elite tier includes 24/7 phone with a designated success rep. Average ticket resolution is under 10 minutes.
How does pricing scale as we grow?
Per user, per month, no surprise tier jumps. As you add engineers you add JobWatch licences, that's it. The platform itself doesn't get more expensive because you're billing more invoices or scheduling more jobs.
